# Product Requirements Document (PRD): Disalignment.com

## 1. Executive Summary & Objective
**Disalignment.com** is a top-of-funnel educational platform designed to bridge the "missing middle" in AI communication—the gap between impenetrable academic jargon and sensationalist, apocalyptic media tropes [1, 2]. 

The primary objective is to serve as a pedagogical engine and an "invisible filtration mechanism" [3]. It must educate the general public using engaging, phenomena-driven storytelling, and then subtly funnel the most intellectually curious, high-signal users toward **The Witness Protocol** (an advanced AI alignment framework requiring human ethical datasets) [4, 5]. The platform's tone is "a field guide, not a manifesto" [6].

## 2. Target Audience & Positioning
*   **The Audience:** The intellectually curious general public ("mixed priors") who want to understand AI beyond the hype [6]. 
*   **The Positioning:** Rebrand "AI Safety" into "Disalignment." Move away from paralyzing "doomerism" and sterile academic theory toward an investigative, curiosity-driven exploration of systemic flaws [7, 8].

## 3. UI/UX & Aesthetic Requirements (The "Noir Detective")
*   **Color Palette:** Monochromatic dark mode. Pure black (`#000000`) and deep charcoal (`#121212`) backgrounds with stark white typography [9]. 
*   **Visual Style:** "Cinematic scientific visualization." Avoid corporate "tech blue" and abstract connecting nodes [10]. Use parallax scrolling to symbolize looking "beneath the surface" of AI models [11].
*   **Anti-Sludge:** The site must be the absolute antithesis of deceptive patterns. Maximum whitespace, zero pop-ups, no gamified leaderboards, and no FOMO mechanics [11-13].

## 4. Core Features & Custom Components
1.  **Epistemic Hygiene Badges:** Every major claim must be tagged with a status label: *Well-Established*, *Emerging Evidence*, or *Speculative* [14, 15].
2.  **Universal Changelog:** A block at the bottom of every article detailing "Updated Date," "What Changed," and "Why" to build radical journalistic trust [16].
3.  **MCP-Powered Citation Tooltips:** Hover-cards over technical terms that fetch definitions and source citations dynamically via the NotebookLM MCP server.
4.  **Interactive "Explorable Explanations":** 
    *   *The Shoggoth Toggle:* A visual component allowing users to toggle between a "Smiley Face" (RLHF / masked behavior) and the alien "Shoggoth" (raw base model) [17-19].
    *   *Choose-Your-Own-Assumptions:* Sliders that adjust timelines, model autonomy, and deployment constraints to demonstrate how expert opinions vary [20].

## 5. Information Architecture & The Invisible Funnel
The site acts as a 4-stage invisible funnel, progressively increasing cognitive demand [21, 22]:

*   **Top of Funnel (Awareness):** 
    *   *Phenomena-Driven Hooks:* Mysteries like "Why Did the AI Ram the Boat?" to explain Reward Hacking/Specification Gaming [23-25].
*   **Middle of Funnel (Consideration):** 
    *   *Conversational Deep-Dives:* Long-form essays (using the *Wait But Why* model) explaining complex concepts like Instrumental Convergence and the Waluigi Effect [26-28].
    *   *Serious Games:* Interactive browser modules demonstrating race-to-the-bottom dynamics [29, 30].
*   **Bottom of Funnel (Evaluation):** 
    *   Complex explorations of ASI containment, cryptographic cosignatures, and the need for human ethical datasets [31].

    *   A hidden or softly promoted gateway page. As the user enters, **all entertaining noir elements are stripped away**, shifting to an austere, minimalist presentation [32, 33].
